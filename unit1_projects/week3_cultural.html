My Style of Thinking

by Mark McQuillen

June 1, 2014

Thinking 'Bout Thinking

My Thinking Style as suggested by the thinking style quiz is a mix of Abstract Random and Concrete Random. One characteristic of this thinking style is a strong desire for healthy relationships. I agree I often learn best when in community and in dialogue. For that reason, I will try to keep the lines of communication flowing when I don't understand something and need help or when I have something to share.

I believe original ideas often come into being by merging two often very different ideas. In a sense, the partnering is random. Since I have a tendency toward random thinking, I will do my best to offer creative solutions and creative project proposals when working with a team.

I will utilize my study of Nonviolent Communication to try to hear fully the feelings, needs, and requests of people I am working with and to express myself in a compassionate way.

The biggest struggle for me in Phase 0 has been applying what I have read. For this reason, I have reached out to my accountability group and cohort when I do not understand something. I think part of my difficulty comes from the fact that so much of this information is completely new to me so it's hard for me to integrate it into a broader understanding. I believe as I learn more about coding, learning about coding will become easier because I will have a stronger foundation and greater context for new knowledge.

I am committed to having a growth mindset. For this reason, I am open to expressing my ignorance and asking for help when I need it. I'm also trying to apply organizational techniques to bring more structure to how I work. Related to the structure of how I work, I recently downloaded a Pomodoro app so I can bring better focus to my work time by allocating break time. I'm using it right now!
