Knowing Thyself Prevents Misunderstandings (with Thyself)

by Mark McQuillen

August 10, 2014

Release 1: When you think of the times in your life where you have been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, which of the following values come to mind?

Accomplishment, Caring, Peace, Environmentalism, Humor, Romance

Release 2: Pick one value and write a couple of sentences about why one of the values you chose is important to you.

I care about peace because humanity faces a number of urgent social justice and environmental challenges, and I believe we will need to engage in peaceful and productive dialogue to address these challenges. That is why I am a student on Nonviolent Communication. NVC offers strategies of communication with a core emphasis on empathy and cooperation.

Release 3: In general, I try to live up to these values: 4.5

Disagree 1 ----- 2 ----- 3 ----- 4 ----- 5 Agree

Release 4: What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on?

Several people asked for my advice last night as I ate a burrito on the bench outside the new El Barrio Burritos restaurant in Brooklyn. They wanted to know if I was enjoying the burrito and if I recommended they get one. I told them, I like burritos and it is a burrito, so I am enjoying it. If you like burritos, get a burrito.

Release 5a: How do you feel when you think about your values?

I feel grounded and centered.

Release 5b: Do you think it can help you mediate stereotype threat if you recognize you feel it?

Maybe. It might remind me of who I am at the core rather than the stereotypes others or myself might assign to me. I will have to give it a try.

Release 5c: Can you think of any other exercises you can use to help you when you are feeling down (for whatever reason) at DBC?

Yes. Exercises. Like jumping jacks, jogging in place, jogging in the park, pushups, sit-ups. Anything to get the blood pumping and the breath moving. Help clear out the system. Also, embracing the beauty of nature can bring a sense of wellness.
